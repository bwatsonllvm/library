About This Website

This website is a searchable index of LLVM Developers' Meeting talks and meeting metadata from 2007 to present. It brings titles, speakers, abstracts, tags, and links to videos or slides into one place so you can quickly find sessions by key topic, person, or year.

Use the search bar, filters, and meetings view to explore the archive. URLs capture active filters, so specific views can be bookmarked or shared. Talk detail pages include full abstracts and direct links to available resources.

Data is compiled from the public meeting archive and packaged for fast browsing in this static interface. For canonical listings and announcements, see the official meeting archive.

How to Use This Site

  • Search — Type any keyword in the search box to find talks by title, speaker, or key topic. Quoted phrases (e.g. "register allocation") are treated as exact matches. Results are ranked by relevance and recency.
  • Filter by category — Use the chip strip below the search box to narrow results to Keynotes, Technical Talks, Tutorials, Lightning Talks, and more.
  • Filter by year — Click a year chip to show only talks from that meeting year.
  • With Video / With Slides — Toggle these to surface only talks with a recording or slide deck available.
  • Browse by meeting — Visit the By Meeting page for a full grid of every LLVM Developers' Meeting since 2007, with talk counts. Click any meeting to browse all of its talks.
  • Browse papers — Visit the Papers page to explore academic papers hosted on llvm.org, including authors, tags, and abstracts.
  • Talk detail page — Click any talk card to open a detail view with the full abstract, embedded video (when available), slides link, and related talks from the same meeting or category.
  • Shareable links — Active search queries and filters are reflected in the URL, so you can bookmark or share any filtered view directly.
  • Display settings — Use the sliders menu in the top-right corner to set theme (System, Light, or Dark) and text size (Small, Default, or Large). Preferences are saved across visits.
